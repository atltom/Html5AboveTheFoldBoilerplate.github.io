Types of Pavers for Your Walkway

Choosing the right type of paver is crucial for the look and longevity of your walkway. Here are some of the most popular types of pavers we use:

Concrete Pavers

Concrete pavers are a popular choice due to their affordability and versatility. They come in a wide range of colors, shapes, and textures, making them ideal for almost any home design. Concrete pavers are also durable and low-maintenance, standing up well to harsh New England winters.

Brick Pavers

Brick pavers are classic and timeless, offering a more traditional look for your walkway. They are strong, durable, and come in a variety of colors, giving you a range of options for customizing your walkway. They pair beautifully with homes that have a colonial or rustic design.

Natural Stone Pavers

For a more elegant and luxurious look, natural stone pavers such as flagstone, bluestone, or granite are ideal. These pavers offer a unique, one-of-a-kind look as each stone varies in color and texture. They are highly durable and weather-resistant, making them perfect for New England's varying seasons.

Permeable Pavers

Permeable pavers allow water to flow through the joints, reducing water runoff and promoting sustainability. They are an excellent choice for homeowners in Concord looking to manage stormwater and reduce their environmental footprint.

Popular Paver Patterns for Walkways

The pattern you choose for your paver walkway can drastically alter the look and feel of your outdoor space. Here are some popular paver patterns we recommend:

Running Bond Pattern

This classic pattern is perfect for a clean, straightforward look. It involves laying pavers in rows, with each paver aligned with the one next to it, creating a sleek, linear design.

Herringbone Pattern

The herringbone pattern is highly dynamic and adds a touch of elegance to your walkway. It involves laying pavers at 45-degree angles, creating a "V" shape that draws attention and adds visual interest.

Basketweave Pattern

This pattern alternates pavers in a weaving design, creating a more intricate and timeless appearance. Basketweave is often chosen for its traditional feel and is ideal for homes with classic architecture.

Circular Patterns

For a more sophisticated and bold look, circular patterns add complexity and charm to any walkway. This design is typically used in areas like garden entrances or around fountains, where you want to create a focal point.

Ashlar Pattern

The ashlar pattern combines large and small pavers in a random pattern, offering a more natural and organic look. This is ideal for homeowners who want a walkway that looks like it evolved over time rather than being designed.
